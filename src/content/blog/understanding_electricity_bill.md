## Decoding the Bill: It’s Not Just "Per Unit"

Many people think, "I pay ₹8 per unit." It is rarely that simple. Your bill has four main villains:

### 1. Telescopic Tariff Slabs
MSEDCL charges you differently based on how much you use.
*   **0–100 units:** Cheap (e.g., ~₹5.88).
*   **101–300 units:** Moderate (e.g., ~₹11.46).
*   **301–500 units:** Expensive (e.g., ~₹15.72).
*   **500+ units:** Very Expensive (e.g., ~₹17.81).

*Note: Rates vary slightly by financial year, but the logic remains.*

**The Trap:** If you use 350 units, you don't pay the high rate for all 350. But those top 50 units are costing you **3x more** than your base units. Looking at the "Average Billing Rate" often hides this pain.

### 2. Fuel Adjustment Charge (FAC)
This is a variable charge added per unit based on the cost of coal/fuel. It fluctuates but always adds up.

### 3. Electricity Duty & Tax on Sale
This is a percentage tax on top of your energy charges. So when your rate goes up, your tax goes up too. It’s a double whammy.

### 4. Fixed Charges
You pay this just for having a meter. It increases if your "Sanctioned Load" is higher.
